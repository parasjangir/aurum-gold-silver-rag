# Silver — Purity, Rates, and Buying

> Educational sample for Aurum. Verify hallmarking and tax details against
> official BIS / GST sources.

## Silver purity grades

Silver purity is expressed as fineness in parts per thousand (BIS grades):

- **999** — 99.9% pure (fine silver), soft; used for coins/bars and investment.
- **925** — 92.5% silver, known as **sterling silver**; the standard jewellery
  grade, alloyed (usually with copper) for durability.
- **835** — 83.5% silver.
- **800** — 80.0% silver.

Sterling silver (925) is the most common for jewellery because it balances shine
with strength. Pure 999 silver is too soft for most ornaments.

## Hallmarking of silver

BIS also hallmarks silver. A silver hallmark shows the BIS mark and the fineness
grade (e.g. 925). As with gold, a hallmark certifies the purity so the buyer
isn't sold a lower grade than stated.

## Silver rate (bhav)

Silver is much cheaper per gram than gold, so it is often quoted **per kilogram**
as well as per gram. Like gold, the local silver rate is driven by the
international price, the USD–INR rate, taxes, and demand (industrial demand
matters more for silver than for gold). Aurum reads today's silver bhav from the
rates the jeweller sets.

## Pricing silver items

Silver articles are priced much like gold:

    final = (silver rate per gram × weight) + making charges + applicable GST

Making charges apply to silver jewellery and artware too, and vary with design.
Heavy items like utensils, idols, and gift articles are common in silver.

## Uses of silver

- **Jewellery** — sterling (925) rings, chains, anklets ("payal"), toe rings.
- **Investment** — 999 coins and bars.
- **Articles / gifting** — utensils, idols, photo frames, pooja items.
- **Industrial** — electronics, solar, and more (a big source of silver demand).

## Buying tips

- Look for the BIS hallmark and the fineness grade (e.g. 925 for sterling).
- For investment, prefer 999 coins/bars over jewellery to avoid making charges.
- Silver tarnishes over time; it can be cleaned/polished and re-shined.
