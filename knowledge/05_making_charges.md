# Making Charges & Wastage on Gold Jewellery

> Educational sample for Aurum. Figures are typical ranges, not fixed rules —
> making charges are set by each jeweller and are negotiable.

## What making charges are

Making charges (also called labour or "ghadai" charges) are the fee for the
craftsmanship of turning raw gold into a finished ornament. They are charged
**on top of** the metal value and are one of the biggest reasons two shops quote
different totals for the same weight of gold.

## How making charges are quoted

There are two common structures:

1. **Per gram (flat):** a fixed rupee amount per gram, e.g. ₹400–₹800 per gram.
   Common for plain, machine-made items like chains and bangles.
2. **Percentage of metal value:** typically 8%–25% of the gold value. Common for
   designed, handcrafted, or stone-studded pieces. Intricate and antique-finish
   work sits at the higher end; simple items at the lower end.

Lightweight or highly detailed items tend to carry higher percentage making
charges because the labour is high relative to the gold used.

## Wastage charges

"Wastage" is an additional charge (often expressed as a percentage of weight,
e.g. 2%–8%) meant to cover gold lost during crafting. In modern practice wastage
is frequently bundled into making charges. Always ask whether wastage is charged
separately or already included, so you are not paying for it twice.

## Why making charges vary so much

- **Design complexity** — handwork, filigree, and antique finishes cost more.
- **Machine vs handmade** — machine-made (CNC, casting) is cheaper than handmade.
- **Brand** — large branded showrooms often charge higher making charges than
  local jewellers, partly for design and service.
- **Negotiation** — making charges are negotiable, especially on bigger purchases
  and during festive offers (some shops waive or discount them).

## Buyer tips

- Ask for making charges to be shown **separately** on the bill, not merged into
  a single per-gram "all-inclusive" rate that hides them.
- Compare the *effective* making charge across shops (convert per-gram and
  percentage quotes to the same basis before comparing).
- On resale or exchange, making charges are usually **not** recovered — you get
  paid for the gold content, not the labour. So high making charges are a real,
  largely non-recoverable cost.
