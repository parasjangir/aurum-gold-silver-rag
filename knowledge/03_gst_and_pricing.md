# How Gold Jewellery is Priced (and Taxed) in India

> Educational sample for SonaRAG. Tax rates change — always confirm current GST
> rates with a chartered accountant or the official GST portal before relying on them.

## The price build-up

The final bill for a gold ornament is built from several parts:

1. **Metal value** = gold rate per gram (for the relevant karat) × net weight in grams.
2. **Making charges** — the jeweller's labour/craftsmanship fee. Charged either
   as a flat amount per gram or as a percentage of the metal value.
3. **Stone / diamond value** — added separately for studded jewellery.
4. **Hallmarking charge** — a small per-article fee for BIS hallmarking.
5. **GST** — Goods and Services Tax, applied on top.

A simplified formula:

    final price = (rate × net weight) + making charges + stone value + GST

## Net weight vs gross weight

For studded jewellery, the **gross weight** includes stones, while the
**net weight** is the gold alone. Reputable billing charges the gold rate only
on net gold weight and prices stones separately. Always check that you are not
paying the gold rate on the weight of the stones.

## GST on gold jewellery (Sarafa method)

In everyday Sarafa Bazar / jeweller billing, GST is applied as **3% on the
total** of metal value + making charges. You compute the metal value and the
making charges, add them into a subtotal, then add 3% GST on that subtotal.

(For reference, the statutory structure is levied on the gold value; this sample
follows the common shop practice of a flat 3% on the billed subtotal. Confirm
current rates with a CA or the GST portal.)

## Making charges vary

Making charges differ by design complexity. Machine-made plain chains carry low
making charges; intricate, handcrafted, or antique-finish pieces carry higher
ones. Because making charges are negotiable and design-dependent, two shops can
quote very different totals for the "same" weight of gold.

## A worked example (Sarafa method)

A 12 g 22K ring, Sarafa 22K rate ₹14,230/g, making charge 11%:

- Per-gram price = rate + (rate × 11%) = 14,230 + 1,565.3 = ₹15,795.3
- Subtotal = 15,795.3 × 12 g = ₹1,89,543.6
- GST = 3% × 1,89,543.6 = ₹5,686.31
- **Final price = ₹1,95,229.91**

Always use the directly-quoted Sarafa rate for the exact karat (22K = ₹14,230/g
here) — don't derive the 22K rate from the 24K rate.
