# How to Read a Hallmark and Verify It

> Educational sample for SonaRAG. Confirm verification steps via the official BIS
> CARE app and bis.gov.in.

## Finding the hallmark on the piece

Hallmarks are tiny laser marks, usually placed on a discreet but findable spot:
the clasp of a chain or necklace, the inner band of a ring, or the post/back of
an earring. A jeweller's loupe (magnifier) helps you read them clearly.

## Decoding the three marks

On current (HUID-era) gold hallmarks you will see:

- The **BIS triangular logo**.
- The **purity grade**, e.g. `22K916`, `18K750`, or `14K585`. The first part is
  the karat; the number is the fineness in parts per thousand.
- The **six-digit alphanumeric HUID**, unique to that one article.

If a purity grade and karat disagree (for example `22K` printed next to `750`),
that is a red flag — 22K should read 916, not 750.

## Verifying the HUID

Because every hallmarked article has a unique HUID stored in the BIS database,
you can verify a specific piece rather than trusting only the printed mark. The
BIS CARE mobile app lets a buyer enter the HUID and check the registered
details — including the registered jeweller and the article's purity. A HUID
that does not resolve, or whose details do not match the piece in hand, should
prompt questions before purchase.

## Common buyer mistakes

- Assuming any stamp is a BIS hallmark. Decorative or jeweller-applied stamps
  are not the same as a BIS hallmark with a valid HUID.
- Not checking that the gold rate was applied to **net** weight on studded items.
- Ignoring making charges, which can swing the final price substantially.

## Quick checklist before buying

1. Is there a BIS logo, a purity grade, and a HUID?
2. Do the karat and the fineness number agree (22K ↔ 916, 18K ↔ 750)?
3. Does the HUID verify in the BIS CARE app with matching details?
4. Is the bill itemised — metal value, making charges, stones, GST — separately?
