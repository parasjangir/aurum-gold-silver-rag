# Buying, Selling & Investing in Gold and Silver

> Educational sample for Aurum. Not financial advice — verify scheme details and
> current rules before investing.

## Forms of gold you can buy

| Form | What it is | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Jewellery** | Ornaments (22K/18K) | wearing + holding value; carries making charges |
| **Coins / bars** | 24K bullion from jewellers/banks | pure-gold holding; low/no design cost |
| **Digital gold** | Gold bought online, stored in vaults | small amounts, convenience |
| **Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)** | Govt securities priced to gold | long-term investment; pays interest, no storage |
| **Gold ETFs** | Exchange-traded funds tracking gold | demat investment, easy to trade |

## Jewellery vs investment gold

Jewellery carries **making charges** (largely non-recoverable on resale) and is
usually 22K. If the goal is purely investment, coins/bars (24K), SGBs, or ETFs
avoid making charges and are closer to the pure metal value. If you want to wear
it, jewellery makes sense — just treat the making charges as a cost of ownership.

## Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) — key points

- Issued by the RBI on behalf of the Government of India.
- Priced in grams of gold, so the value tracks the gold price.
- Pay a fixed annual interest on the invested amount, on top of price movement.
- No making charges, no storage, no purity worries.
- Have a multi-year maturity, with rules on early exit.

## Selling, buyback and exchange

- On resale, you are paid for the **gold content** (purity × weight × rate), not
  the making charges. High-making-charge pieces lose that labour cost on resale.
- Many jewellers offer **buyback** or **exchange** — bringing old gold against a
  new purchase. Exchange often recovers more value than an outright cash sale.
- A valid **BIS hallmark / HUID** helps on resale because the purity is certified,
  reducing disputes and deductions.
- Bills and certificates matter — keep them; they ease resale and exchange.

## Practical tips

- For pure investment, prefer 24K coins/bars, SGBs, or ETFs over jewellery.
- For jewellery you'll wear, minimise non-recoverable cost by negotiating making
  charges and choosing hallmarked pieces.
- Always get an itemised bill (metal value, making charges, GST shown separately).
