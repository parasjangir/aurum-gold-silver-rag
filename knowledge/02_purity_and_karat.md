# Gold Purity, Karat, and Fineness

> Educational sample for SonaRAG. Verify figures against official BIS standards.

## Karat explained

Karat (K) measures gold purity as parts out of 24. Pure gold is 24 karat.
The karat number tells you how many of 24 parts of the alloy are gold:

- **24K** — 99.9% gold (fineness 999). Pure gold, very soft.
- **23K** — about 95.8% gold (fineness 958).
- **22K** — 91.6% gold (fineness 916). The most common grade for Indian gold jewellery.
- **18K** — 75.0% gold (fineness 750). Popular for diamond and stone-studded jewellery.
- **14K** — 58.5% gold (fineness 585).
- **9K** — 37.5% gold (fineness 375).

Fineness is just purity expressed in parts per thousand: 22K = 916/1000.

## Why jewellery is rarely 24K

Pure 24K gold is too soft to hold intricate designs or set stones securely; it
bends and scratches easily. Jewellers alloy gold with metals such as copper,
silver, and zinc to add hardness and durability. The alloying metals also
shift the colour — more copper gives a warmer, reddish tone; more silver gives
a paler tone. This is why 22K and 18K, not 24K, dominate everyday jewellery.

## Computing pure-gold content

To find the pure-gold weight in an article, multiply the gross gold weight by
the fineness factor:

    pure gold (g) = gross weight (g) × (karat ÷ 24)

For example, a 10 g 22K chain contains 10 × (22 ÷ 24) = 9.16 g of pure gold.

## Silver purity grades

Silver is also hallmarked by BIS, by fineness in parts per thousand:

- **990** — 99.0% silver (fine silver).
- **925** — 92.5% silver, known as sterling silver; the common jewellery grade.
- **835** — 83.5% silver.
- **800** — 80.0% silver.

As with gold, the remaining fraction is alloy added for strength.
