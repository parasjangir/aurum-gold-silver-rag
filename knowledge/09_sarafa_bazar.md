# Sarafa Bazar & the Sarafa Traders Committee

> Educational sample for Aurum. The live rates Aurum uses come from the rates the
> jeweller sets (from the local committee's daily sheet), not from this document.

## What Sarafa Bazar is

"Sarafa" refers to the bullion / precious-metals market. In most Indian cities a
local **Sarafa Traders Committee** (Sarafa Association) publishes the day's gold
and silver rates. These committee rates are the reference that jewellers across
the city use for the day — when someone asks "aaj ka bhav kya hai?", they mean
the Sarafa rate.

## How the daily sheet is quoted

A typical committee sheet (e.g. Jaipur) lists, for that date:

- **Gold (approx)** — an approximate pure/24K reference rate, per gram.
- **Silver (approx)** — silver, per gram (silver is also often quoted per kg).
- **22KT, 18KT, 14KT** — the per-gram rate for each karat, quoted **directly**.
- A **return** line — e.g. "22KT-18KT-14KT RETURN 500 PER GM LESS" — meaning on
  buyback you are paid ₹500 per gram **less** than the buying rate.

The important practice: use the **directly-quoted per-karat rate** (for example
22KT at ₹14,230/g). Do not compute the 22K rate from the 24K rate — the committee
already quotes it.

## Why rates change every day

Sarafa rates track the international gold price (USD/oz), the USD–INR exchange
rate, import duty, and local demand. Because all of these move daily, the
committee re-issues the sheet each day, and jewellers update their boards
accordingly.

## How this connects to pricing

Once you have today's Sarafa per-karat rate, the price of an ornament is:

    per-gram price = karat rate + (karat rate × making%)
    subtotal       = per-gram price × weight (g)
    final          = subtotal + 3% GST on the subtotal

So the Sarafa rate feeds straight into the making-charge and GST calculation to
give the final billed amount.
